# Momentum Wellness Outreach Tracking Workflow

Use the shared onboarding tracker for early outreach until a dedicated CRM or
sales tool is needed. This keeps the business simple while lead volume is low.

Do not store private health details, payment information, passwords, signed
forms, food logs, progress photos, or sensitive personal information in the
tracker.

## When To Create A Tracker Record

Create a record when:

- A person submits the contact form and needs follow-up.
- Someone replies to individual coaching outreach.
- A company contact expresses interest in employee wellness.
- A consultation needs to be scheduled.
- A proposal or follow-up is pending.

Do not create a record for every person who merely sees a post, likes a post,
or receives an outreach message.

## Record Types

Use these categories:

- Individual coaching lead
- Corporate wellness lead
- General inquiry
- Manual admin entry

## Recommended Statuses

### Individual Leads

- `Needs review`
- `Contacted`
- `Waiting for client`
- `Trainerize pending`
- `Active`
- `Closed`
- `Lost`

### Corporate Leads

- `Needs review`
- `Contacted`
- `Consultation pending`
- `Consultation scheduled`
- `Qualified`
- `Proposal pending`
- `Proposal sent`
- `Won`
- `Lost`
- `On hold`

## Required Fields

For individual leads:

- Name
- Email
- Source
- Status
- Owner
- Next action
- Next action date

For corporate leads:

- Company name
- Contact name
- Business email
- Employee count when known
- Industry when known
- Source
- Status
- Owner
- Next action
- Next action date

## Note Guidelines

Good tracker notes:

- `Sent individual program reply; waiting for response.`
- `Asked for consultation availability.`
- `Consultation scheduled for Tuesday.`
- `Proposal needs internal review before sending.`
- `No response after second follow-up; revisit next month.`

Do not write:

- Medical history
- Readiness form answers
- Payment details
- Private client concerns
- Passwords
- Signed form contents
- Full email threads

## Follow-Up Timing

Suggested timing:

- Respond to new inquiries within one business day whenever practical.
- Follow up after three business days if there is no reply.
- For corporate leads, follow up again one week later if appropriate.
- Move inactive leads to `On hold`, `Lost`, or `Closed` rather than leaving
  them open indefinitely.

## Early Outreach Review

Review the tracker weekly and ask:

1. Which leads need action?
2. Which leads are waiting on Momentum Wellness?
3. Which leads are waiting on the contact?
4. Which leads should be closed?
5. What questions are people asking repeatedly?
6. What website or email copy should be improved?

## When To Upgrade To A CRM

Consider a dedicated CRM only when:

- Lead volume becomes hard to manage manually.
- Multiple people are regularly working leads.
- Follow-up reminders are being missed.
- More reporting is needed.
- Email automation has been reviewed and approved.
