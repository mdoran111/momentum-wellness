# Momentum Wellness Corporate Sales Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook provides a consistent, human-reviewed process for corporate
wellness inquiries. It supports the internal onboarding tracker, corporate
calculator, and proposal generator.

Corporate pricing remains private and consultation-based. Do not collect
employee health information during the sales process or promise medical,
financial, productivity, retention, insurance, or healthcare-cost outcomes.

## Lead Intake

Create a company record in the shared onboarding tracker when a qualified
inquiry is received.

Minimum information:

- Company name
- Contact name
- Business email
- Approximate employee count
- Industry
- Primary wellness goal
- Lead source
- Assigned owner
- Next action and next action date

Do not enter employee names, health details, medical information, payment
information, or other sensitive information in the tracker.

## Consultation Preparation

Before the call:

1. Review the submitted inquiry and check for duplicate company records.
2. Confirm the contact's role and likely involvement in the decision.
3. Review the company website for basic public context.
4. Prepare the consultation questions below.
5. Do not prepare final pricing before scope and employee count are understood.

## Consultation Questions

Use these as a conversation guide rather than a rigid script. Record concise
business notes only.

### Company And Decision Process

1. What does your company do, and where are employees located?
2. Approximately how many employees may be eligible to participate?
3. Are employees primarily onsite, remote, hybrid, or distributed?
4. Which roles or departments would be involved?
5. Who else will participate in evaluating or approving the program?
6. Is there an established budget owner or benefits decision maker?

### Goals And Current Approach

7. What prompted you to explore employee wellness now?
8. What would you like the program to support for your workforce?
9. What wellness resources or benefits are currently available?
10. What has worked well in previous wellness efforts?
11. What has made participation or consistency difficult?
12. Are there specific engagement, morale, energy, or culture priorities?

Do not ask for individual diagnoses, medical histories, or employee health
records.

### Participation And Delivery

13. What schedules, locations, or workforce conditions should the program
    accommodate?
14. How would employees typically access an app-based benefit?
15. What communication channels are available for launch and reminders?
16. Would participation be offered company-wide or to a defined eligible
    group?
17. Is there a preferred launch date or business milestone?
18. How long would you like an initial program to run?

### Support, Reporting, And Privacy

19. What level of implementation and ongoing support would be useful?
20. What employer-level information would help you evaluate participation?
21. Are there internal privacy, legal, security, or procurement requirements?
22. Who should receive approved employer-level reports?
23. How often would leadership want program updates?

Employer reporting should focus on approved aggregate engagement information.
Do not promise access to individual health information.

### Fit And Next Steps

24. What would make this program feel worthwhile to the company?
25. Are there competing priorities or timing concerns we should understand?
26. What is the company's target process for reviewing a proposal?
27. What questions or concerns need to be resolved before moving forward?
28. What is the clearest next step after this conversation?

## Qualification Standard

Mark a lead `Qualified` when:

- The company generally fits the 50-or-more-employee target.
- A legitimate business contact is engaged.
- A clear workforce goal or business need has been identified.
- The company is open to an app-based program.
- Timing and decision process are reasonably understood.
- There is a defined next step.

Use `On hold` when the fit may be valid but timing is not active. Use `Lost`
when the company declines, chooses another option, or clearly does not fit.

## Proposal Preparation

Use the internal proposal generator only when enough information exists to
complete:

- Company name and industry
- Employee count
- Decision maker
- Primary goals
- Proposed program length
- Implementation considerations
- Reporting and privacy expectations

Use the corporate calculator privately to evaluate investment scenarios. Do
not send the calculator or expose internal pricing assumptions publicly.

Every proposal requires internal review before sending. Confirm scope,
investment, implementation, reporting, support, and terms before treating the
proposal as final.

## Lead Stages And Timing

### Needs Review

- Use immediately after intake.
- Review and assign an owner within one business day.

### Contacted

- Initial response has been sent.
- If there is no reply, follow up after three business days.

### Consultation Pending

- The contact has expressed interest but no date is scheduled.
- Offer times and follow up after three business days.

### Consultation Scheduled

- Confirm the date, attendees, and meeting method.
- Send a reminder one business day before the meeting when appropriate.

### Qualified

- Discovery confirms a reasonable fit and defined next step.
- Begin proposal preparation within two business days unless another timeline
  was agreed.

### Proposal Drafting

- Gather required inputs and use the internal tools.
- Do not send before internal review.

### Internal Review

- Confirm claims, scope, investment, implementation, reporting, privacy, and
  terms.
- Resolve open issues before sending.

### Proposal Sent

- Record the date sent and schedule a follow-up for three business days later.

### Follow-Up

- Follow up at three business days and again approximately seven days later.
- After two unanswered follow-ups, move the lead to `On hold` unless there is
  a reason to continue active outreach.

### Won

- Confirm approval, agreement, owner, implementation timeline, and next
  operational handoff.
- Do not collect employee health information in the sales tracker.

### Lost

- Record a concise business reason when known.
- Do not use shame-based or aggressive follow-up.

### On Hold

- Record the reason and an agreed or reasonable reconnection date.
- Review inactive opportunities monthly.

## Tracker Use

Every active company record should have:

- One assigned owner
- One current status
- One clear next action
- One next action date
- Concise, non-sensitive business notes

The tracker is the operational source of truth for follow-up. Email remains the
source of truth for message content, and approved agreements remain the source
of truth for final commercial terms.
