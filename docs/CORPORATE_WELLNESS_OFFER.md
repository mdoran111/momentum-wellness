# Corporate Wellness Offer

## Offer Summary

Momentum Wellness provides company-wide employee wellness programs for
companies with 50 or more employees through the Momentum Wellness app.

The program includes training, nutrition, habits, accountability, community,
progress tracking, company-wide engagement, consultation-based setup, and
employer-level reporting. It is built around workforce health, energy, morale,
accountability, and community.

## Ideal Customer

Current public positioning targets companies with 50 or more employees across:

- Offices
- Healthcare
- Logistics
- Manufacturing
- Trades

The offer may suit organizations experiencing low energy, inconsistent health
habits, limited accountability, high stress, poor recovery, or disconnected
teams. Do not assert that these issues exist at a prospect's company without
discovery.

## Core Program Pillars

### Training

Structured strength, cardio, and mobility programming for different experience
levels and training environments.

### Nutrition

Practical nutrition awareness and guidance intended to support energy and
sustainable choices. Do not describe this as medical nutrition therapy unless
qualified professionals and an approved clinical scope are involved.

### Habits

Support for repeatable behaviors involving sleep, hydration, movement,
recovery, and routine.

### Accountability

App-based tracking, check-ins, and structured follow-through intended to
improve participation and consistency.

### Community

Team messaging, shared participation, and community support intended to
strengthen engagement and culture.

## Delivery Process

The current proposed process is:

1. Consultation: understand company goals, workforce, and needs.
2. Program setup: configure the wellness structure in the app.
3. Company launch: onboard employees and provide access.
4. Engagement: support participation through programming, tracking, and
   community.
5. Reporting: provide approved employer-level visibility into program
   engagement and progress.
6. Ongoing support: help the organization maintain momentum.

Timelines, implementation responsibilities, reporting, support hours, and
employee eligibility require owner approval before they are promised.

## Intended Outcomes

Use language such as "designed to support," "can help," or "the program focuses
on."

Approved outcome themes include:

- Better engagement with health and wellness
- More consistent exercise and daily habits
- Greater nutrition awareness and energy
- Stronger team accountability
- A more connected wellness culture
- A practical wellness benefit employees can use

Never guarantee health, financial, productivity, retention, insurance, or
absenteeism outcomes.

Do not promise healthcare-cost savings.

## Differentiators

- Structured programming rather than an unorganized video catalog
- One app for the core wellness system
- Scalable delivery across locations and schedules
- Suitable for beginners and experienced participants
- Accessible at home, in a gym, or on the go
- Focused on practical participation and long-term habits

## Sales Call to Action

Primary CTA: **Book a Corporate Wellness Consultation**

The consultation may gather:

- Contact name and work email
- Company name
- Role
- Approximate employee count
- Workforce type or locations
- Goals and current challenges
- Desired timing

Avoid collecting employee health information through the sales form.

## Pricing and Terms

Corporate pricing is not currently public. Keep it consultation-based until the
business owner approves:

- Package structure
- Minimum contract value
- Per-employee or flat-rate pricing
- Implementation fees
- Contract length
- Renewal and cancellation terms
- Support scope
- Reporting scope
- Privacy and data-processing obligations

Corporate wellness pricing must not be displayed publicly on the website.

## Proof and Compliance

Before adding claims or proof, verify the source and permission for
testimonials, customer logos, participation rates, health outcomes, ROI
statistics, and case studies.

Corporate deployments may introduce privacy, employment, benefits, and
health-data considerations. Obtain appropriate review before collecting
employee-level wellness information or sharing participation data with an
employer.

Employer-level reporting must protect individual privacy and must not expose
personal health information without a lawful, documented basis.
