# Momentum Wellness Brand Guide

## Purpose

Momentum Wellness is an app-based fitness, nutrition, habit, accountability,
and community wellness company.

The brand helps people and workforces pursue meaningful health transformation
through repeatable systems and long-term momentum. It is not an extreme,
intimidating, or generic fitness brand.

## Core Positioning

Momentum Wellness is an app-based wellness coaching system built around five
connected pillars:

1. Training
2. Nutrition
3. Habits
4. Accountability
5. Community

The primary audiences are:

1. Individuals who want app-based training, nutrition structure, habit
   coaching, accountability, progress tracking, and community support.
2. Companies with 50 or more employees that want employee wellness programs
   built around workforce health, energy, morale, accountability, and
   culture.

## Brand Promise

Provide clear structure, accessible tools, and ongoing support that help people
make sustainable progress over time.

## Personality

The brand should feel:

- Premium
- Professional
- Modern
- Clean
- Trustworthy
- Health-transformation focused
- Practical
- Distinct from a generic fitness brand

Avoid sounding repetitive, generic, aggressive, clinical, fear-based,
shame-based, overly corporate, or like a guaranteed-results fitness scheme.

## Voice and Tone

Use direct, plain language. Emphasize clarity, consistency, support, and
long-term progress.

Preferred themes:

- Build momentum
- Sustainable progress
- Structure and accountability
- Practical behavior change
- Better health habits
- A system people can actually use
- Training, nutrition, habits, accountability, and community

Avoid:

- Guaranteed outcomes
- Rapid transformation promises
- Medical claims
- Healthcare-cost savings promises
- Fear- or guilt-based language
- Unsupported statistics
- Claims that every participant will achieve the same result
- Fake testimonials or client results
- Overhyped fitness language
- Fear-based or shame-based messaging

## Visual Direction

The current website establishes this visual system:

- Primary dark navy: `#0f172a`
- Deeper navy: `#0a0f1d`
- Secondary slate: `#1e293b`
- Primary accent: emerald
- Secondary accent: teal
- Corporate accents may use blue
- Light sections use near-white slate backgrounds such as `#f8fafc`
- Headings use Montserrat
- Body copy uses Inter

Visual characteristics:

- Premium dark backgrounds
- Emerald-to-teal gradients
- Soft glow and restrained texture
- Rounded cards and buttons
- App screenshots presented in phone frames
- Generous spacing and strong typography
- Motion that supports hierarchy without distracting

## Messaging Hierarchy

1. Explain who the offer is for.
2. State the problem in practical terms.
3. Present the relevant parts of the five-pillar system without repeating the
   same section on every page.
4. Show how the app makes the system accessible.
5. Explain what is included and how it works.
6. Use one clear call to action.
7. Set realistic expectations and avoid guarantees.

## Trust Rules

- Treat health, pricing, privacy, legal, and outcome claims as sensitive.
- Never publicly display private client information, progress photos, food
  logs, medical details, or private purchase data.
- Do not invent testimonials, credentials, client counts, partnerships, or
  results.
- Do not present illustrative testimonials as verified customer statements.
- State that outcomes depend on participation and consistency.
- Keep a medical disclaimer available in applicable legal or program contexts.
