## Proposal For

**Company:** Mesa Vista Manufacturing  
**Industry:** Manufacturing  
**Employee Count:** Approximately 120 employees  
**Decision Maker:** Jordan Lee  
**Program Length:** 12 months  
**Primary Goals:** Workforce energy, morale, consistency, accountability, and
practical wellness support.

## 1. Executive Summary

Momentum Wellness proposes an app-based employee wellness program designed to
support healthier habits, stronger daily structure, and practical
accountability across Mesa Vista Manufacturing's workforce.

The program brings training, nutrition, habits, accountability, and community
into one modern platform. It is designed to be approachable for employees with
different schedules, fitness backgrounds, and starting points.

The goal is not to promise a specific health outcome. The goal is to create a
clear, repeatable system employees can use to build consistency over time.

## 2. Company Wellness Opportunity

Mesa Vista Manufacturing is evaluating wellness support for a workforce that
needs practical tools, accessible structure, and a program that can meet people
where they are.

Common workforce priorities may include:

- Helping employees build sustainable health habits
- Supporting energy and morale
- Creating a shared wellness culture
- Making healthy routines easier to follow
- Providing accountability without making the program feel clinical or
  intimidating

Momentum Wellness can support these priorities through a structured app-based
program and employer-level engagement process.

## 3. Momentum Wellness Solution

Momentum Wellness provides company-wide access to a wellness app that organizes
training, nutrition, habit tracking, progress tools, check-ins, and community
support in one place.

The program is designed to support:

- Practical daily participation
- Clear expectations
- Accessible structure
- Repeatable routines
- Accountability and engagement
- Long-term behavior change

The program can be launched with consultation-based setup, employee
communication support, and employer-level reporting focused on aggregate
engagement rather than individual health details.

## 4. The Five Pillars

### Training

Structured fitness programming that supports consistency and progressive
participation.

### Nutrition

Practical nutrition guidance and awareness without medical claims or rigid
prescriptive dieting.

### Habits

Simple habit tracking around routines that support healthier daily behavior.

### Accountability

Check-ins, progress tools, and structure that help employees stay engaged.

### Community

Shared momentum and support to make the wellness process feel less isolated.

## 5. App-Based Program Overview

Employees access the Momentum Wellness program through the app. Depending on
the approved scope, the app can support:

- Training plans
- Habit goals
- Nutrition awareness
- Progress tracking
- Check-ins
- Community messaging
- Program reminders
- Engagement visibility

The app-based approach helps keep the program organized and accessible without
requiring employees to attend the same location or session at the same time.

## 7. Reporting And Privacy

Momentum Wellness reporting should focus on approved employer-level engagement
information. The program should not share individual medical details, health
histories, readiness responses, payment information, or private client records
with the employer.

Any reporting scope should be reviewed before launch and aligned with the
company's privacy expectations.
